## Why note-making is the single most important skill?

Even if you are caveman, you would be way better off with few notes about weather cycles, hunting grounds, poisonous plants on your cave walls. If you are an aspiring novelist, scientist, mathematician, leader, entrepreneur, engineer, a tech worker or a half way decent student trying to understand things (note: I said *understand things* not pass exams) or a monk pondering about enlightenment the importance of note making can't be stressed enough.

### Three crucial skills.

- collecting, organizing and filtering large amount of data.
- connecting the dots and recognizing the patterns.
- Arriving at insights and understanding similarities across domains (forming analogies and categories).

Of the above 3 skills, note-making stands at the core. The other 2 skills build on top of the first skill.

### Second brain and story of a fish.

What sets you apart from a fish in the bowl :) wrt to cognition? Let's assume a fish manages to come up with a basic insight about life, it barely remembers the insight next day. The fish gets a cognitive reset every day and it's back to square one. We can build on our insights/learnings. Google: "Second Brain". Unlike our animal cousins we can afford to have huge repository of insights and searchable data stored outside our brain! Here comes the whole industry of "Personal Information Management".

### Being a good student.

To be a good student we need few ingredients:
- commitment to understand reality, learn it and profit from it!

Once you cleared the above basic requirement, next comes.
- note making (Google: Mind maps)
- revising the notes periodically (Google: "spaced repetition", anki)
- repetition and practicing the skill

Next comes the skills of (and the list grows!)
- asking the right questions (Google: Peter Drucker effective).
- executing the ideas/thoughts etc.

Unfortunately students churned out by schools/colleges don't even have this basic road map and skills training.

### Let's have it too!

Napolean had it, Newton had it, Einstein had it, Darwin had it, Fermat had it, Leonardo da Vinci had it, Richard Branson have it!

All had an active journal of ideas. Journal of ideas and opportunities. (Disclaimer: Alright having a journal doesn't gurantee a Nobel prize)


### A digital note making setup

Here are few things you can try

- Microsoft Onenote 
- wikidpad (a desktop wiki)
- Dokuwiki (a server side wiki)
- Emacs + org mode + git repos + html exports

There are ton of other note-making tools, PIM (Personal Information Managers), do your research and pick what fits your work flow.

### Emacs + org mode + git repos + html exports

This is my favorite setup. This takes time but still worth it. It achieves the following
- plain text based notes (Works on any device/OS etc. no vendor lock in or your data stored in cloud)
- easily sync-able with other devices.
- with html exports the text files can be rendered prettily too !

Good luck with your note-making!
